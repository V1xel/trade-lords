# Lady Isolde the Merchant Princess

## Identity

**Name**: Lady Isolde the Merchant Princess
**Title**: Mistress of the Trade Routes
**House Colors**: Purple & Silver
**Archetype**: Trading Master

## Appearance

Elegant noblewoman in traveling cloak of purple silk, ornate coin purse, merchant's ledger, jeweled circlet.

## Background

Inherited her father's merchant fleet and expanded it into the largest trading empire in the realm through shrewd negotiations.

## Ability: "Trade Mastery"

### Mechanics

**Merchant Advantage**:
- Starts with 5 merchants (instead of standard 2)
- Can unlock up to 6 total (standard is 6 maximum)

**Shipping Bonus**:
- Starts with river-crossing shipping level for free

**Trade Bonus**:
- Gains £1 every time trading at the Market
- Applies to both buying and selling
- Applies to each trade action

### Game States Modified

**Player State**:
- `merchants_available: 5` (starting, vs standard 2)
- `shipping_level: 1` (river-crossing, vs standard 0)

**Trade Action**:
- Execute normal buy/sell
- Add £1 to player gold

### Examples

**Example 1: Multiple Trades**
- Isolde has 5 merchants available
- Round 1: Buys Wool, Sells Grain, Buys Milk
- Uses 3 merchants
- Gains £3 bonus (£1 per trade)
- Has 2 merchants remaining for the round

**Example 2: Compared to Standard Lord**
- Standard lord: 2 merchants, can make 2 trades per round
- Isolde: 5 merchants, can make 5 trades per round
- Isolde generates £5 extra gold just from trading

**Example 3: Shipping Advantage**
- Turn 1: Isolde can cross rivers immediately
- Other lords must pay £4 to unlock river-crossing
- Enables early expansion across water

## Strategy

### Strengths
- Dominant market presence
- High trade frequency
- Free gold generation from trading
- Early expansion via free shipping
- Economic engine

### Weaknesses
- Ability doesn't help with production
- Still needs units to produce goods
- Merchant saturation (5 is often more than needed early)
- No direct production bonuses

### Playstyle

**Early Game**:
- Exploit shipping to claim distant valuable terrain
- Trade frequently for £1 bonuses
- Manipulate market prices aggressively
- Build diverse production for trade flexibility

**Mid Game**:
- Continue high-frequency trading
- Use gold advantage for rapid expansion
- Control market through volume trading
- Unlock 6th merchant if needed

**Late Game**:
- Convert gold advantage to victory points
- Trade surplus goods for needed resources
- Maintain market manipulation
- Fulfill contracts with purchased goods

### Key Decisions
- **What to Trade**: Balance price manipulation vs immediate needs
- **Merchant Usage**: When to hold merchants vs trade
- **Shipping Routes**: Leverage early shipping for expansion

### Synergies
- Benefits from any production type (can trade anything)
- Strong with diverse terrain access
- Complements contract strategies (buy missing goods)

### Counters
- Market volatility from other players
- Opponents avoiding trades
- Limited terrain despite shipping

## Starting Resources

- **Gold**: £12
- **Units**:
  - 1 Sheep
  - 1 Cow
- **Merchants**: 5 (special)
- **Shipping**: 1 (river-crossing, free)

## Difficulty

**Complexity**: Beginner
**Reason**: Straightforward ability, immediate benefits, extra resources provide safety

## Victory Paths

1. **Market Dominance**: Control prices, high-volume trading, gold accumulation
2. **Expansion**: Use shipping + gold for wide territory control
3. **Flexible Contracts**: Buy needed goods rather than producing them
